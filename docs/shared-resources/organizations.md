# Shared Organizations Documentation

## Overview

Shared organizations are institutional bodies, governing structures, and professional networks accessible to both Academic and Entertainment career systems. These entities facilitate collaboration, establish policies, and create connection points between the two systems.

## Organization Summary

| Name | UID | Type | Systems | Primary Function |
|------|-----|------|---------|-----------------|
| Arts and Education Council | `org_arts_council` | Governing | Both | Institutional governance and policy |
| Student Union | `org_student_union` | Student | Both | Student representation and activities |
| Alumni Network | `org_alumni_network` | Professional | Both | Professional connections and mentorship |

---

## Organization Details

### 1. Arts and Education Council

**UID**: `org_arts_council`

**Type**: Governing

**Oversees**: Academic, Entertainment

**Description**: 
Joint governing body for academic and entertainment programs. Strategic leadership body that sets institutional policies, allocates resources, and coordinates cross-system initiatives.

**Mission Statement**:
"To advance education and artistic excellence through integrated governance, resource sharing, and collaborative innovation while maintaining system independence and fostering institutional growth."

**Organizational Structure**:

```
Arts and Education Council
├── Executive Committee (8 members)
│   ├── Chair (rotating, annual)
│   ├── Academic Director (Dean or Chancellor)
│   ├── Entertainment Director (Studio Head or Executive Producer)
│   ├── Finance Officer
│   ├── Operations Officer
│   ├── Community Relations Officer
│   ├── Innovation Officer
│   └── Student Representative
├── Academic Subcommittee
│   ├── Academic track leadership
│   ├── Faculty representatives
│   └── Student academics representative
├── Entertainment Subcommittee
│   ├── Entertainment track leadership
│   ├── Performance representatives
│   └── Student entertainment representative
├── Budget Committee
│   ├── Finance leadership
│   ├── Academic allocations
│   ├── Entertainment allocations
│   └── Cross-system initiatives
└── Strategic Planning Committee
    ├── Long-term vision
    ├── Resource development
    ├── Market analysis
    └── Sustainability planning
```

**Responsibilities**:

1. **Governance**
   - Policy development for both systems
   - Institutional strategic planning
   - Compliance oversight
   - Risk management
   - Legal and ethical guidance

2. **Resource Allocation**
   - Budget distribution ($5M+ annually)
   - Facility scheduling and use
   - Equipment procurement
   - Personnel decisions
   - Capital projects

3. **Cross-System Initiatives**
   - Bridging program development
   - Joint event planning
   - Collaborative funding
   - Technology integration
   - Research partnerships

4. **External Relations**
   - Community engagement
   - Alumni relations
   - Industry partnerships
   - Donor relations
   - Media relations

5. **Performance Metrics**
   - System effectiveness measurement
   - Student/performer satisfaction
   - Financial performance
   - Community impact
   - Progress toward strategic goals

**Career System Integration**:

*Academic Access*:
- Researcher (Level 4) - Advisor participation
- Professor (Level 5) - Active member (1 seat)
- Department Head (Level 6) - Regular member
- Dean (Level 7) - Primary representative (3 seats)
- Chancellor (Level 8) - Executive authority (1 seat, rotating chair)

*Entertainment Access*:
- Director (Level 5) - Advisor participation
- Producer (Level 6) - Active member (1 seat)
- Executive Producer (Level 7) - Regular member
- Studio Head (Level 8) - Executive authority (1 seat)

**Annual Activities**:
- 4 executive meetings (quarterly)
- 12 full council meetings (monthly)
- 4 subcommittee meetings per committee (quarterly)
- Annual strategic planning retreat
- Annual budget development cycle
- Annual community forum
- Regular public reporting

**Budget Authority**:
- Academic system funding: ~$3.2M annually
- Entertainment system funding: ~$1.8M annually
- Cross-system initiatives: ~$0.3M annually
- Capital reserves: $2M+ for major projects

**Notable Initiatives**:
- Student-Performer Exchange Program
- Interdisciplinary Production Initiative
- Educational Entertainment Series
- Joint Conference Series (annual)
- Mentorship Program (cross-system)
- Innovation Grant Program
- Community Outreach Projects

**Meeting Locations**:
- Primary: University Campus, Administrative Center
- Alternate: Performance Hall boardroom
- Virtual: Hybrid meeting capability

### 2. Student Union

**UID**: `org_student_union`

**Type**: Student

**Serves**: Academic, Entertainment

**Description**: 
Student organization representing both academic and entertainment interests. Democratically governed body advocating for student needs, organizing activities, and facilitating peer connection across systems.

**Mission Statement**:
"To represent student interests, foster community, provide quality programming, and advocate for student success in both academic and entertainment pursuits."

**Organizational Structure**:

```
Student Union
├── Executive Board (8 elected officers)
│   ├── President
│   ├── Vice President (Academic)
│   ├── Vice President (Entertainment)
│   ├── Treasurer
│   ├── Secretary
│   ├── Social Coordinator
│   ├── Communications Officer
│   └── Grievance Officer
├── Representative Assembly (30+ elected student reps)
│   ├── Academic representatives (20)
│   ├── Entertainment representatives (10)
│   └── Class representatives
├── Standing Committees
│   ├── Finance (budget oversight)
│   ├── Programming (event planning)
│   ├── Advocacy (student issues)
│   ├── Diversity & Inclusion
│   ├── Sustainability
│   └── Facilities
└── Elected Leadership
    ├── Budget allocation
    ├── Policy development
    ├── Event approval
    └── Student advocacy
```

**Membership**:
- All academic students: Automatic membership (Levels 1-3)
- All entertainment students: Automatic membership (Levels 1-3)
- Faculty advisor: 1 academic, 1 entertainment
- Staff support: Executive director + staff

**Member Services**:

1. **Advocacy**
   - Student concern representation
   - Administrative appeals
   - Policy feedback channels
   - Grievance resolution
   - Student rights protection

2. **Programming**
   - Weekly social events
   - Cultural celebrations
   - Academic support workshops
   - Performance showcases
   - Networking events
   - Community service projects

3. **Networking**
   - Cross-system mixer events
   - Club and organization support
   - Mentorship opportunities
   - Professional development
   - Internship coordination

4. **Resources**
   - Free study space in union building
   - Computing resources
   - Printing facilities
   - Lounge areas
   - Food service
   - Event planning support

**Budget and Funding**:
- Student activity fee funding: $500K+ annually
- Fee allocation: 60% academic, 40% entertainment
- Club grant program: $100K available
- Event funding: $150K annually
- Special initiatives: $50K for new programs

**Annual Programming**:

- **Winter Break**: Holiday celebration, winter festival
- **Spring Semester**: Spring break programming, end-of-year events
- **Year-Round**: Weekly social hours, networking events

**Notable Programs**:

- Academic Excellence Workshops

- Leadership Development Training

**Elections**:

- Voting: All student members eligible

**Communication**:

**Meetings**:

### 3. Alumni Network

**UID**: `org_alumni_network`

**Type**: Professional

**Includes**: Academic, Entertainment

**Description**: 
Professional network connecting graduates from both career paths. Organization facilitating career advancement, mentorship, networking, and lifelong connection to institutional community.

**Mission Statement**:
"To foster lifelong connections among graduates, support professional development across careers, and strengthen the institutional community through alumni engagement and mutual support."

**Organizational Structure**:

```
Alumni Network
├── National Leadership (30 members)
│   ├── President
│   ├── Board of Directors (8)
│   ├── Executive Director (paid staff)
│   └── Regional coordinators
├── Chapter System (15+ chapters)
│   ├── Geographic chapters (by region)
│   ├── Career-based chapters
│   └── Industry-specific chapters
├── Affinity Groups
│   ├── Academic professionals
│   ├── Entertainment professionals
│   ├── Cross-career professionals
│   └── Mentor groups
└── Supporting Staff
    ├── Communications
    ├── Events
    ├── Membership
    └── Development
```

**Membership**:
- All chancellors and studio heads: Automatic leadership

- Entertainment mentors: Director+
- Graduating students: Automatic alumni status

- Life membership fee: $250-500 (varies)

4. **Giving and Support**

### Cross-Organization Collaboration

#### Arts and Education Council ↔ Student Union

- Special initiatives funded jointly
- Leadership shared experience

#### Student Union ↔ Alumni Network
- Student members become alumni members
- Alumni mentors through student programs
